PRML Solutions Manual

A complete, chapter-by-chapter solutions manual for Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning (PRML) by Christopher Bishop. This site provides detailed derivations, worked examples, and mathematical explanations.

Start reading: Chapter 1 – Introduction →

Chapters

Each chapter link includes a short description to help readers and search engines understand the content.

  • Chapter 1 – Introduction: overview of pattern recognition and machine learning.
  • Chapter 2 – Probability Distributions: key probability models used throughout PRML.
  • Chapter 3 – Linear Models for Regression: regression models and least squares.
  • Chapter 4 – Linear Models for Classification: logistic regression and discriminant functions.
  • Chapter 5 – Neural Networks: multilayer perceptrons and backpropagation.
  • Chapter 6 – Kernel Methods: kernel trick and feature spaces.
  • Chapter 7 – Sparse Kernel Machines: relevance vector machines and sparsity.
  • Chapter 8 – Graphical Models: directed and undirected probabilistic models.
  • Chapter 9 – Mixture Models and EM: Gaussian mixtures and expectation–maximization.
  • Chapter 10 – Approximate Inference: variational methods and message passing.
  • Chapter 11 – Sampling Methods: MCMC and sampling-based inference.
  • Chapter 12 – Continuous Latent Variables: PCA, factor analysis, and latent variable models.
  • Chapter 13 – Sequential Data: HMMs, Kalman filters, and time-series models.
